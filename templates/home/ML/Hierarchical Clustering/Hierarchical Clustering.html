Hierarchical Clustering

We have a hierarchy of clusters formed under this algorithm.

Suppose we have 4 datapoints - P1,P2,P3,P4. Within them P1 & P2 are most similar points so they are grouped under a same cluster. And they are grouped under a bigger cluster.

We can represent the hierarchy of the clusters using a diagram as shown called Dendogram. In the dendogram we can see that P1 & P2 are most similar and then they are similar with P3. And their combination is similar with P4. We have to keep on forming clusters until we are left with only 1 big clusters and all the datapoints are covered.

It is subdivided into 2 types->

  1. Agglomerative= We need down to up approach of dendogram.
  2. Divisive= We need up to down approach of dendogram.
